Steve Jobs (1955-2011) was an American entrepreneur and business magnate who co-founded Apple Inc. He was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, and passed away on October 5, 2011, in Palo Alto, California. Jobs was widely recognized for his visionary leadership and his role in revolutionizing the personal computer industry, mobile technology, and the way we consume music and media. He was known for his uncompromising design aesthetic, attention to detail, and ability to create groundbreaking products that combined technology and artistry.

Sundar Pichai, born on July 12, 1972, is an Indian-American business executive and the CEO of Google Inc. and its parent company, Alphabet Inc. He was born in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India, and completed his education at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, where he earned a Bachelor's degree in Metallurgical Engineering. Pichai later pursued further studies in the United States, earning a Master's degree in Material Sciences and Engineering from Stanford University and an MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.
